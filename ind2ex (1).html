How this document works:

This document is divided into two layers throughout... essentials and additionals.

The essentials are the main body of the document. They're what you see with every collapsible section collapsed. They are genuinely enough... what periods are, what happens, what takes place, how to help, everything.

The additionals are the collapsible orange sections you'll see between paragraphs. They contain the how and the why... from basic mechanisms of the ovary to going as far out of topic as explaining how neurons fire and what their resting voltage is (negative 70 millivolts). Read them only if you're obsessively passionate about understanding this stuff. So if I say alcohol affects menstruation and you're struggling with both drinking and menstruation, it's worth expanding that additional section to understand what's going on and why.

A few controls you should know about:

There's a small menu button at the top right corner. Inside it you'll find three toggles:

  • Light Mode... switches between dark and light theme.
  • Show Headings... the document has topic headings scattered through it. Turn this off for a continuous reading experience with no divisions, or keep it on to see where one topic ends and another begins.
  • Expand Additionals... opens or collapses every additional section at once. By default they're collapsed.

You can also tap any individual orange bar to expand just that one section.

The thin bar at the very top of your screen is a progress bar... it tracks how far you've scrolled. If you expand additional sections, it adjusts because there's now more to read.
